Food

Hong Kong has a huge number of dining options that with world of exquisite, mouth-watering.First of all, restaurants are found everywhere in Hong Kong for sure, espescially Chinese restarants, you can found a lot of outstanding restarants in major hotels and shopping complexes. Most specialise in: Cantonese, Chiu Chow, Peking, Shanghainese, Szechuan and Hunan, Chinese Vegetarian or Chinese Festive Foods and Chinese Wine.

Hong Kong is an international city that many cultures and tastes are represented in Hong Kong's world of dining. Visitor will enjoy five-star cuisine, fast-food, snacks or casual family-style meals in Hong Kong's Eastern and Western fabulous restaurants. Other restaurants from all over the world such as Thai, Vietnamese, Indian, Indonesian, Malaysian and Filipino restaurants are somehow affected and have some revolutions from Chinese food, as do Japanese sushi bars and fine restaurants. The Western restaurants are also can be found everywhere in the Hong Kong city districts, they are well represented with American, French, Italian, Mediterranean and other cuisines for those with a penchant for western fare or Mexican makes for a spicy alternative.

"Not only is Hong Kong the Culinary Capital of Asia but also a place where you can dine in style with incredible views, overlooking the harbour, or savour the joy of Hong Kong signature dishes and regional Chinese delicacies while gazing at the stunning skyline. You'll love all that Hong Kong cooks up. " - Hong Kong Discover website

Seafood

As a city located near the ocean, one food must be in the "must try" list of Hong Kong cuisines - seafood. Huge tanks of live fish and bubbling containers of prawns, crabs, scallops, clams, oysters and other seafood are attractively make by the master cheafs wiith hundred differet ways. Preparing fresh, live seafood is a culinary art in Hong Kong, live seafood markets are located everywhere, and of course, the skilled Cantonese chefs will have you salivating over the distinctive flavours and textures of their fishy dishes.

There are four prime seafood locations for waterfront dining "C Sai Kung, Lei Yue Mun, and the islands of Lamma and Cheung Chau. So don't miss the great opportunity to join the lively atmosphere of alfresco dining Hong Kong style, and savour a gourmet meal, this is sure to be one of the high points of your visit.

Cha Chaan Teng

The Hong Kong cha chaan teng is a classic, 1950s to 1960s style coffee shop diner. The menu is like an anthropological record of Hong Kong's post-war manufacturing boom. The interior decor is usually tacky. The English on the menus, when available, is quite entertainingly bad. The food is inexpensive and tasty, although not the healthiest stuff around. An eclectic mix of Hong Kong and Canto-Western cuisine, the restaurants are a definitive part of Hong Kong's culture. Thanks to Markham's Chinese population, they're now part of the GTA too.

An authentic cha chaan teng in Hong Kong has the same sort of charm as an esoteric crawlspace pizza joint snuggled somewhere deep in the belly of the metropolis, the kind with bona fide Italians with questionable affiliations gesturing passionately from behind grubby counters - the kind with, undeniably, the very best pizza. A unique mix of several types of black tea and evaporated milk, this signature drink always comes free with the afternoon specials and is a quintessential part of the cha chaan teng experience. The name translates to "tea restaurant". what makes cha chaan teng special are the sheer number of menu options. Meal choices include absolutely inestimable variations of baked spaghetti, congee, sizzling meat plates, soup noodles, fried rice, sandwiches, and much, much more. "Happy Hour" or "Afternoon Tea" specials offer especially large portions of food for unbelievably low prices.

Restaurants from other Countries

Hong Kong is a gourmet's paradise that every Western flavour imaginable can be found.The question is not merely what you are going to eat, but where, as your taste buds can easily transport you to any other international culinary region. French, Italian and German fare is widespread, along with Greek, Mexican and American-style cuisine. The city prides itself on its gourmet restaurants, pizzerias and American-style fast-food outlets. Chic cafes, intimate bistros, gourmet sandwich bars and delicatessens are the perfect place to relax and enjoy some wonderful cuisine.

The bonus in many of Hong Kong's Western restaurants is their memorable location. Try, for example, Victoria Peak or a table overlooking a fishing harbour. Tantalise your senses with a bird's-eye panoramic views of Victoria Harbour, cosy downtown basements or modern street-level restaurants.

Hong Kong is the best place in the world to sample the dazzling variety of all Asian cuisine. The diversity of Asian influences in Hong Kong is reflected though the range of fantastic food. Try the spices of Thailand; the rich aromatic flavours of India, the delicacies of Japanese, Korean and Vietnamese cuisine, and also a wide range of halal cuisine. The cultures converge in Hong Kong, where it's possible to find flavours to suit every palate. The diverse, intense South East Asian flavours are widely available throughout Hong Kong. Expatriate residents from every Asian country contribute their own distinctive cuisine to this cosmopolitan city.
