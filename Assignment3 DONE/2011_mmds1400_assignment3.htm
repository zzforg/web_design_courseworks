MMDS1400/7961Assignment 3:

Overview

The teams from Assignment 2 will now work together to develop the website they designed and tested.

This assignment is worth 25% of the course.

Deadline

  • 9am Monday 23rd May (Week 12)

Goals

The purpose of this assignment is to give students an experience in implementing a website using current web technologies, working as a team.

Requirements

You are to develop a subset of the website you designed in Assignment 2. You must develop a minimum of 9 webpages up to a maximum of 15 webpages. This must include the homepage of course.

  1. Content: Make sure that all of the necessary content and images have been included in the web pages that your team have developed. Check these carefully for spelling and grammatical errors and copyright permission.
  2. Development: The pages should feature:
    • XHTML: All of the pages your team develops must feature semantic and appropriate XHTML. This MUST be validated.
    • CSS: All of the pages your team develops must be styled with a valid CSS2 document. This MUST be validated.
    • JavaScript: At least three distinct uses of Javascript must be used with the site you develop.
    • PHP: At least two distinct uses of PHP must be used in the site you develop.
  3. Functionality: Ensure that your website has been validated and is fully functional. Carefully check the following:
    • Images: Are all of your images displaying correctly and do they feature appropriate alt tags?
    • Links: Are all of links and navigation items working and correct?
  4. Design: Ensure that the pages which you have developed are well designed and consistent. Consider the following:
    • Cohesion: Make sure that your pages are consistent with the designs that were prototyped in Assignment 2.
    • Colour and Typography: Ensure that your colour scheme and typography choices are appropriate and do not effect the readability or accessibility of your website.
  5. Plagiarism: Ensure that you have acknowledged the copyright of all material on your website EVEN if you are the copyright holder.
  6. Accessibility: Make sure your website is still functional with all images disabled, CSS removed and Javascript disabled.

Submission

Assignment is to be submitted via the web assignment submission page located at:

http://submit.itee.uq.edu.au/

The submission of your work should bea single Zipped file containing all the XHTML, CSS, PHP and Javascript documents and necessary files (e.g. graphics if any) in appropriate folders, which you developed for or used in this assignment. You must include your team information (e.g. team name and team member names and their student number) in the main page.

Zipped assignments are to be named as MMDS1400_TeamName_assignment_3.zip, for example MMDS1400_T1a_assignment_3.zip. Students in shadow course (MMDS7961) are to submit in the same way. Remember to include your team name, student names and student number on the main page. Pay close attention to the format of the submission name, remembering the number and occurrence of underscores. Also the file limit is 15Mb, remember to scale or re-size images using an image manipulation package such as Adobe Photoshop, the GIMP or similar.
